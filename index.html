01 · About

About Me

I'm a PhD student at the SMILE Lab, CLSP at Johns Hopkins University, advised by Dr. Berrak Sisman. My research targets a critical vulnerability in speech security: state-of-the-art anti-spoofing models break down when faced with emotionally expressive attacks, because they rely on dataset-specific artifacts rather than generalizable speech structure.

To address this, I built EmoSpoof-TTS — the first TTS-based emotional synthetic speech dataset — and proposed GEM, an emotion-aware detection framework. I also designed HuLA and ProSDD, human-perception-inspired prosody-aware models achieving up to 86% EER reduction on expressive attacks.

Beyond security, I work on speech for healthcare (depression and Alzheimer's detection) and speech synthesis. Before Hopkins, I completed my MS at UCLA under Dr. Abeer Alwan, focusing on ASR for child speech.

03 · Research

Research Areas

🎤
Speech Security & Anti-Spoofing
Identifying failure modes in SOTA deepfake detection. Built EmoSpoof-TTS and proposed GEM, HuLA, and ProSDD — emotion-aware frameworks achieving up to 86% EER reduction on expressive attacks.
Core Focus
🧠
Speech for Healthcare
Models for depression and Alzheimer's detection from speech. Studying speaker leakage in depression detection to ensure clinical reliability.
Active
🔊
Speech Synthesis & Voice Conversion
Released NaturalVoices (5,000+ hours), a large-scale spontaneous and emotional podcast dataset for voice conversion. Expressive TTS systems.
Active
